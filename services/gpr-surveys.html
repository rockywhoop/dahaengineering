Ground Penetrating Radar (GPR) Surveys

Non-destructive subsurface investigation using advanced radar technology

Service Overview

Daha Engineering provides state-of-the-art Ground Penetrating Radar (GPR) surveys for non-destructive subsurface investigation. GPR technology uses electromagnetic waves to detect and map buried utilities, voids, and structural anomalies without excavation, making it ideal for infrastructure assessment, utility location, and pavement analysis.

Comprehensive GPR Survey Services

Subsurface Utility Mapping

Precise detection and mapping of buried utilities including power lines, water mains, gas pipes, and telecommunications cables without excavation.

Void & Cavity Detection

Identification of underground voids, cavities, and subsurface anomalies indicating potential structural hazards or unstable ground conditions.

Pavement Layer Analysis

Non-destructive evaluation of road pavement structure showing layer thickness, base condition, and identifying delamination or deterioration.

Concrete Structure Assessment

Evaluation of concrete thickness, rebar location, voids, and structural integrity without damaging existing structures or surfaces.

Groundwater Table Detection

Identification of groundwater levels and subsurface water saturation patterns critical for construction and foundation design planning.

Soil Stratum Profiling

Characterization of subsurface soil layers, bedrock identification, and geological stratum boundaries for geotechnical assessment.

Buried Pipe Localization

Detection and depth determination of underground pipes including sewage, irrigation, and drainage systems for maintenance and repair planning.

Mine & Collapse Detection

Identification of underground mines, sinkholes, and collapse zones indicating ground instability and subsidence risk.

Archaeological Surveys

Non-invasive detection of buried archaeological features, artifacts, and structures for heritage site investigations.

Material Characterization

Analysis of subsurface materials, soil properties, and material composition based on dielectric characteristics detected by GPR.

GPR Data Processing & Interpretation

Advanced processing, filtering, and interpretation of GPR data with detailed reports and color-coded depth profiles.

GIS-Based Utility Mapping

Integration of GPR results with GIS systems for comprehensive utility mapping and spatial database creation.

Technology & Equipment

  • GPR Systems: Multi-frequency GPR equipment (400 MHz to 2 GHz) for varying penetration depths
  • Cart-based Systems: Automated GPR systems for large area surveys with GPS integration
  • Hand-held Units: Portable GPR for precision location work in restricted areas
  • Data Processing Software: Professional GPR interpretation and analysis software
  • GPS Integration: Real-time GPS positioning for accurate utility mapping
  • GIS Export: Direct export to GIS formats for utility database integration
